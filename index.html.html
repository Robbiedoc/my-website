About

The researcher who knows
how reviewers think

I'm a chronic disease and spatial epidemiologist with deep expertise in alcohol and health, violence, tobacco, health equity, and the social determinants of health. My research has been used in communities across California and the US to strengthen efforts on underage tobacco sales, violence reduction, and health disparities.

I've held faculty and leadership positions at Harvard Medical School, Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center, the University of Michigan, and the Prevention Research Center (PIRE). As Scientific and Program Director at Alcohol Justice, I've built and led research programs serving diverse and underserved populations locally and internationally.

What sets me apart: I've sat on grant review panels and know exactly what reviewers look for — and what kills an otherwise strong application. I apply that insider perspective to every proposal I work on. I'm a quick study on new research areas, determined, and committed to doing the best work possible on every engagement.
